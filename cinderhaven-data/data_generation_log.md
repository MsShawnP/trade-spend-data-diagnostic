# Data Generation Log — Cinderhaven Product Master

This document describes how the synthetic dataset was constructed and which defects were intentionally introduced to simulate real-world retail product data conditions. Every defect pattern is drawn from observed conditions in actual specialty food product masters.

The dataset is generated by `scripts/build_db.py` in the cinderhaven-data repository. Individual table generators are documented below in pipeline order.

## Product Master (90 SKUs)

Seeded from `scripts/seed_product_master.sql`. Three product lines of 30 SKUs each: Artisan Sauces, Specialty Condiments, Pantry Staples.

Intentional defects:

- 9 of 90 SKUs (10%) have invalid GTIN-14 check digits. Rate mirrors observed human data-entry error rates in mid-market food companies where GTIN fields are manually keyed, not scanned or calculated.
- 3 of 90 SKUs have invalid UPC-12 check digits. Lower rate than GTIN-14 because UPC-12 is printed on the physical package and more likely to be copied correctly.
- 17 of 90 SKUs (19%) have missing or placeholder brand owner values (literal "NA" string). Simulates the common pattern where the field exists in the schema but was never populated because it wasn't required at initial setup.
- 6 of 90 SKUs have missing country of origin.
- 29 of 90 SKUs (32%) have null case dimensions and/or case weight. Simulates the gap between what retailers require and what gets entered during initial product setup, when the packaged product may not physically exist yet.
- 42 of 90 SKUs (47%) have implausible case weights. These are non-null but unrealistic values, simulating data entry without physical verification.
- 14 distinct serving size strings across 90 SKUs, including casing inconsistencies ("2 tbsp" vs "2 Tbsp") and conflicting gram equivalents ("1 tsp = 2g" vs "1 tsp = 5g"). Drawn directly from real product master exports where serving size is a free-text field with no validation or controlled vocabulary.
- OneWorldSync statuses distributed as: 35 Not Registered (39%), 29 Registered-Incomplete (32%), 17 Synced (19%), 9 Registered-Complete (10%). Reflects the typical state of a $25M specialty food company that started the 1WorldSync process but never prioritized completion.

## Stores (902)

Generated by `scripts/01_generate_stores.py`.

- Walmart: 500 stores
- Whole Foods: 120 stores
- Costco: 80 stores
- 5 regional chains: Green Basket Market (44), Southside Grocers (44), Prairie Provisions (40), Harbor Fresh (36), Mountain Pantry Co (36)
- UNFI: 1 pseudo-store (aggregate distribution point)
- DTC: 1 pseudo-store (aggregate direct-to-consumer channel)

UNFI and DTC are modeled as single distribution points, not physical retail locations. Velocity calculations at these channels represent total throughput, not per-store rates.

## Distribution Log (12,507 authorizations)

Generated by `scripts/02_generate_distribution.py`.

- Each SKU is authorized at a subset of stores based on product line and retailer fit.
- 80 deauthorizations (0.64% overall rate) distributed with a quality bias: lower data-quality SKUs are deauthorized at higher rates. Bottom-half quality SKUs: 1.18% deauth rate. Top-half: 0.26%. This simulates the observed correlation between data quality and retailer shelf retention.
- DTC-only SKUs (12 of 90) have no retail authorizations. These are products sold only through the direct channel.
- No SKU is authorized at the same store twice.

## Chargebacks (381 events, $88k over 18 months)

Generated by `scripts/02b_generate_chargebacks.py`.

- Chargebacks are assigned only to SKU/retailer pairs with active authorization records in the distribution log.
- Chargebacks can only occur on or after the SKU's first authorization month at that retailer.
- Weighted by data quality score: SKUs with more defects receive higher chargeback probability per month.
- Reason distribution: Invalid GTIN/UPC (60%), Dimension mismatch (18%), Missing product data (18%), Late delivery (3%), Short shipment (2%). The three data-defect reasons account for 96% of dollars, matching observed patterns where clerical errors dominate chargeback causes.
- Event amounts follow a lognormal distribution (most events $100-300, occasional events $500+) calibrated against published retailer penalty structures.
- Monthly variance is built in: not every eligible SKU/retailer pair generates a chargeback every month. The monthly probability is tuned to produce an annualized run rate of $55-75k.
- Pareto concentration: 5 SKUs generate 50% of dollars, 15 generate 80%. Mirrors the extreme concentration observed in real chargeback ledgers.

## SKU Costs (90 rows)

Generated by `scripts/03_generate_costs.py`.

- Gross margin range: 35% to 55%, mean 44%. Consistent with specialty food wholesale margins.
- No SKU has negative margin (COGS never exceeds wholesale price).
- Wholesale prices range from $3.61 to $10.56 per unit.

## Promotions (198 rows)

Generated by `scripts/04_generate_promos.py`.

- Distributed across all 10 retailers (4 contracted + 5 regional + DTC).
- Discount depths range from 10% to 50%, mean 23%.
- Each promotion applies to a specific SKU/retailer pair with an active authorization.
- 18 of the generated promotions have sufficient pre-promotion baseline scan data to compute lift. Median observed lift: 74%.

## Price History (398 rows)

Generated by `scripts/04b_generate_price_history.py`.

- Every SKU has at least one price history record.
- Prices are consistent with sku_costs wholesale prices.
- Effective dates are capped at the scan data window end (2026-05-02). No future-dated records.
- Distributed across all 10 retailers with active authorizations.

## Scan Data (1,118,009 rows)

Generated by `scripts/05_generate_scan_data.py`.

- 104 weeks of weekly scan data (2024-05-11 to 2026-05-02).
- Scan data exists only for SKU/store pairs with active authorizations in distribution_log. Zero unauthorized scan records.
- No negative units or revenue.
- Mean velocity: 7.0 units/store/week. UNFI shows higher per-store velocities because it is modeled as a single distribution point aggregating wholesale volume.
- Revenue per unit aligns with wholesale price ranges from sku_costs.

## Bugs found and fixed during this project

Three bugs in the original data generators were discovered during the audit process and corrected:

1. **Chargebacks on unauthorized pairs.** The original `02b_generate_chargebacks.py` assigned chargebacks to all four contracted retailers regardless of whether the SKU was authorized at that retailer. 56% of original chargeback dollars were on unauthorized SKU/retailer pairs. Fixed by adding an authorization lookup filter to both the defect-driven and operational chargeback loops.

2. **Regional retailer mismatch.** `04_generate_promos.py` and `04b_generate_price_history.py` used the string "Regional" as a retailer name, but the stores table uses the five individual chain names. 12 promotions and 27 price history rows were invisible to any retailer-level join. Fixed by expanding "Regional" to the five actual chain names at generation time.

3. **Future-dated price history.** `04b_generate_price_history.py` generated effective dates up to 2027-06-23, over a year past the scan data window. Fixed by capping `effective_date` at 2026-05-02.

These fixes are documented here because they demonstrate the kind of data integrity issues that exist in real product data ecosystems. The fact that a purpose-built synthetic dataset contained authorization-mismatch bugs is itself evidence of how easy these errors are to introduce and how hard they are to catch without a systematic audit.

## Deduction Pipeline

The following tables extend the base dataset with a full retailer deduction lifecycle. Generated by scripts 07–15, run automatically as part of `build_db.py`. All dates are capped at 2026-05-02 to align with the scan_data window.

## Retailers (11 rows)

Seeded from `scripts/seed_deduction_static.sql`.

- Canonical retailer reference linking retailer_id to name, channel_type, and dispute portal details.
- Includes all 6 contracted retailers (Walmart, Costco, Whole Foods, UNFI, KeHE, DTC) plus the 5 regional chains.
- Each retailer has a dispute_portal_url and submission_method reflecting real-world variance (EDI portal, email, web form).

## Retailer Rules (90 rows)

Seeded from `scripts/seed_deduction_static.sql`.

- Per-retailer × deduction-type dispute rules defining window_days, evidence_required, and expected recovery_rate.
- Window days range from 30 to 180, reflecting the wide variance in retailer dispute timelines.
- Recovery rates range from 5% to 85%, with the lowest rates on vague/unsupported deduction types and the highest on short_ship with strong POD evidence.

## Deduction Codes (97 rows)

Seeded from `scripts/seed_deduction_static.sql`.

- Retailer-specific deduction codes mapping internal codes to deduction types.
- Covers real-world patterns: Walmart Code 22 (short ship), Code 25 (compliance), KeHE UDR codes, UNFI allowance codes.
- Each code has a description and maps to a canonical deduction_type used in downstream tables.

## EDI Requirements (42 rows)

Seeded from `scripts/seed_deduction_static.sql`.

- Per-retailer compliance specifications for label, pallet, ASN, and OTIF requirements.
- Includes tolerance thresholds (e.g., OTIF 95% target) and fine amounts per violation type.
- Used by shipment and deduction generators to determine which shipments trigger compliance fines.

## Orders (~5,800 rows) and Order Lines (~30,000 rows)

Generated by `scripts/08_generate_orders.py`.

- Purchase orders from retailers to Cinderhaven over the 104-week window.
- Order frequency varies by retailer: Walmart weekly, regional chains biweekly, Costco monthly.
- Each order contains 3–8 line items drawn from SKUs authorized at that retailer.
- Unit prices are consistent with sku_costs wholesale prices for the respective retailer.
- Order dates are capped at 2026-05-02.

## Pack Records (~5,800 rows)

Generated by `scripts/09_generate_pack_records.py`.

- One pack record per order, documenting label compliance and evidence format/location.
- Label compliance rate: ~88%. Non-compliant packs trigger label_fine deductions downstream.
- Evidence formats: PDF (60%), image (30%), none (10%). The 10% with no evidence creates the "weak evidence" pattern visible in dispute outcomes.
- Evidence locations use realistic paths (S3-style URIs).

## Shipments (~5,800 rows)

Generated by `scripts/10_generate_shipments.py`.

- One shipment per order with carrier, BOL number, delivery date, ASN submission flag.
- Short ship rate: ~6%. Damaged rate: ~3%. Late delivery rate: ~8%.
- ASN compliance: ~85% have valid ASN submitted before delivery.
- Delivery dates are 2–7 days after order date, reflecting LTL shipping to retail DCs.

## Deductions (~3,000 rows)

Generated by `scripts/11_generate_deductions.py`.

- Deduction types: short_ship, label_fine, pallet_fine, damaged, late_delivery, promo_billback, vague, spoilage, slotting.
- Annualized dollar volume: $750K–$1.2M (3–5% of wholesale revenue). Calibrated against published CPG benchmarks for specialty food brands.
- Type distribution: short_ship and promo_billback are the largest categories by dollar volume. Vague deductions (~15% of count) represent the "unexplained" deductions that are common in retailer remittances.
- Slotting fees are one-time charges at authorization, not recurring.
- **Double-dip seeding:** 3 deductions flagged `is_double_dip=1` totaling $15K–$20K. These represent promotions funded via off_invoice (already deducted from the invoice price) that also received a promo_billback deduction — the retailer collected the discount twice. Amounts are $5,800–$7,200 each, consistent with high-volume promotional events.

## Post-Audit Claims (~45 rows)

Generated by `scripts/12_generate_post_audit_claims.py`.

- Retroactive audit clawbacks filed 60–180 days after the original transaction.
- Types: Walmart APL (Account Payable Leverage) and KeHE pass-through audits.
- Each claim references an existing deduction and adds incremental dollar exposure.
- Dates are capped at 2026-05-02; claims that would exceed the window are pulled back by 1–7 days.

## Remittances (~500 rows)

Generated by `scripts/13_generate_remittances.py`.

- Payment events bundling deductions per retailer × week.
- Each remittance aggregates 4–12 deductions from the same retailer within a 7-day window.
- Remittance amounts equal the sum of constituent deduction amounts (no rounding discrepancies).
- Used downstream by the recovery tool to show deduction visibility gaps (deductions buried in bulk payments).

## Disputes (~1,400 rows) and Dispute Evidence (~3,000 rows)

Generated by `scripts/14_generate_disputes.py`.

- Dispute filings covering ~47% of deductions by count. The remainder are undisputed (missed window, insufficient evidence, or triaged as not worth pursuing).
- Outcomes: won (~8%), lost (~62%), pending (~30%). The low win rate reflects the "lean team, mostly handwritten evidence" thesis.
- Evidence quality scores: 1–5 scale. Higher scores correlate with higher win rates but are rare (only 12% of disputes have score ≥ 4).
- Labor hours per dispute: 0.5–4.0 hours. Used to calculate cost-of-dispute vs. recovery ROI.
- Evidence items per dispute: 1–4 documents. Required evidence is determined by retailer_rules; submitted evidence may be incomplete.

## Promotions Extension (promo_cost, funding_mechanism)

Added to the existing promotions table by `scripts/04_generate_promos.py`.

- **promo_cost**: Estimated dollar cost of each promotional event. Calculated as depth × estimated volume × average wholesale price, with ±20% variation. ~5% of events have NULL promo_cost (incomplete data pattern). Populated at ~95% rate across all rows.
- **funding_mechanism**: How the promotion is funded. Values: off_invoice, bill_back, scan_down, mcb (manufacturer chargeback), fixed_fee, slotting. Distribution varies by retailer and promo type:
  - Walmart: primarily scan_down and bill_back
  - Costco: primarily off_invoice
  - UNFI/KeHE: primarily mcb and bill_back
  - Regional chains: mixed, skewing toward off_invoice
  - DTC: fixed_fee only
- The funding_mechanism column enables double-dip detection: off_invoice promotions that also have a promo_billback deduction represent duplicate charges.
