## 1. Dispute submission system(s)

Wegmans operates a vendor-facing supplier portal accessible via `wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers` for purchase order management (DSD, Warehouse, Quick Response), cost maintenance, and ASN entry [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers]. There is no public documentation of a dedicated, named accounts-payable dispute portal in the Walmart/APDP mold. For deduction inquiries, suppliers are directed to the Accounts Payable/Receivable team at `AccountsPayable@wegmans.com` or `(585) 328-2550 ext. 5151`, or to their category merchant/buyer via 1-800-WEGMANS [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/contact-us]. Formal disputes follow a written-notice-then-good-faith-resolution path described in Wegmans' terms, with notices to `legal@wegmans.com` [source: https://www.wegmans.com/terms]. Submission method appears to be email-and-buyer-relationship rather than a self-service dispute UI.

## 2. Deadline windows

Not publicly documented. Wegmans' supplier pages and supplier code of practice do not publish shortage-claim, compliance-fine, or late-delivery dispute windows [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers/supplier-codes-of-practice]. Industry guidance treats 30–60 days from deduction as the typical retailer norm where windows are unpublished, but this is general rather than Wegmans-specific [source: https://supplierwiki.supplypike.com/articles/what-is-a-post-audit-claim].

## 3. Common deduction / chargeback types

No published Wegmans-specific code list was located. Routing-guide and packaging documentation imply enforcement around: SSCC pallet labels (two per pallet, front and side, top third), GS1-128 case labels with required human-readable fields (lot, catch weight, expiration, CoO), 4-way pallet condition standards, carton size/weight limits, and one-PO-per-carton rules [source: https://www.retailerhub.ai/retailer-compliance/wegmans-packaging-guidelines] [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers/additional-shipment-information]. Routing violations on inbound freight are likely (CH Robinson is the routed carrier above 150 lbs; FedEx ground below) [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers/ltl-routing-guide].

## 4. Compliance program

Wegmans publishes a Supplier Codes of Practice, a Vendor Guide, traceability requirements, and a packaging/transportation guide rather than an OTIF-style scorecard [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers]. GFSI certification is required for Wegmans Brand suppliers [source: https://www.wegmans.com/service/for-our-suppliers/supplier-food-safety-and-quality-assurance]. No published OTIF percentage-fine program comparable to Walmart's was found.

## 5. Post-audit behavior

Not publicly documented for Wegmans specifically. Industry-wide, third-party AP auditors (Cotiviti, Connolly, HRG-targeted recovery, etc.) routinely run lookbacks of up to ~3 years against grocery suppliers [source: https://retail.cotiviti.com/solutions/recovery-audit] [source: https://blog.speedylabs.ai/post-audit-deductions/]. Whether Wegmans engages a named third-party auditor is not surfaced in public sources.

## 6. Known quirks

Wegmans is genuinely specialty- and local-supplier friendly at the buyer/category level — buyers visit facilities, use RangeMe for discovery, and pursue long-term, multi-generation supplier relationships [source: https://www.supermarketnews.com/supplier-news/wegmans-takes-supplier-relationships-to-next-level] [source: https://www.rangeme.com/wegmans]. The reputational counterweight: vendors describe an adversarial commercial posture — aggressive trade-deal pressure, ~50% private-label penetration, and willingness to delist competing branded SKUs, with one trade publication summarizing it as "shoppers love it; many vendors don't" [source: https://retailwire.com/discussion/frbuyer-wegmans-shoppers-love-it-many-vendors-dont/]. Wegmans foregoes slotting revenue in favor of private-label shelf space, which is unusual among large chains.
