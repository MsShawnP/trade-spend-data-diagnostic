# UNFI (United Natural Foods) — Deduction & Dispute Research

Source: web research, 2026-05-07. Citations inline; verification gaps
listed at the end. Used as input to synthetic-data generation; do not
treat as legal or compliance authority.

UNFI operates as two largely separate businesses with different portals, teams, and codes: the **Natural** side (legacy UNFI, often called the "Lawson" system) serving Whole Foods, Sprouts, and independent natural retailers, and the **Conventional** side (legacy SuperValu) serving mainstream grocers via the **ePASS** self-service portal [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions] [source: https://tryintercept.com/blog/unfi-deductions]. Cinderhaven, as a specialty food brand into Whole Foods, would primarily deal with the **Natural** side.

## 1. Dispute submission systems

Natural-side disputes are filed by **email only** to `Deductions@unfi.com` using the **UNFI Natural Supplier Dispute Form** (an Excel `.xlsb` template — PDFs and screenshots are rejected). Subject line format: `Dispute: [Brand], [Check #], [Deduction Invoice #], [Dispute Amount]`. Conventional-side disputes are filed through the **ePASS** portal [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions]. **UNFI Insights** is a separate analytics platform that surfaces line-item deduction data on each remittance but is not itself a dispute submission tool [source: https://tryintercept.com/blog/unfi-platform]. To receive MCB backup files weekly, brands must opt in via `supplierdeductiondisputemgmt@unfi.com` [source: https://www.confidotech.com/blogs/manage-deductions-and-disputes-for-unfi].

## 2. Deadline windows

UNFI does not publish a single hard dispute deadline; SupplierWiki recommends submitting "as soon as you have identified an issue," with most disputes acknowledged with a tracking number within two business days and resolved within 30–45 days [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions]. Third-party guides cite a typical 30–60 day practical window [source: https://tryintercept.com/blog/unfi-deductions]. Overage product must be arranged for removal within **14 days** of UNFI notification, and a fill-rate corrective plan is required if shortfalls persist beyond two consecutive weeks [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions]. Could not verify a separate deadline for freight/MCB/slotting categories.

## 3. Common deduction / MCB types

Documented Natural-side categories include: cash discount (2% 10 Net 30), shortage, late delivery ($250+), no-show ($500), short-notice reschedule ($300), recall fees ($3,000 base + disposal), unsaleables/spoils, fill-rate **3% service-level fine** for missing 95% fill two weeks running, **35% off-invoice overship deduction**, freight allowance, slotting, ClearVue program, SSA fee (2.5% of purchases, effective May 1, 2024), and MCB [source: https://www.confidotech.com/blogs/manage-deductions-and-disputes-for-unfi] [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions]. Each carries a three-letter chargeback code; the Natural and Conventional code sets are different [source: https://support.gocrisp.com/hc/en-us/articles/360058227233-UNFI-chargeback-codes] (could not verify the full code list — Crisp's KB returned 403). **Billback vs chargeback**: a billback is the broad family (any post-event deduction the brand owes back), and an MCB chargeback is one specific form of billback for promo discounts UNFI passed through to retailers [source: https://www.govividly.com/blog/deductions-management-tip-no-1-mcb].

## 4. Promo / MCB processes

MCBs are reconciled periodically, with backup arriving weekly if the brand has opted in [source: https://www.confidotech.com/blogs/manage-deductions-and-disputes-for-unfi]. Disputes commonly arise from PO/invoice mismatches, duplicate promo deductions coded under two different reasons, and unsubstantiated promo chargebacks with no paperwork — Woodridge documents an example brand seeing $8,000 in promo chargebacks with no matching backup in a single quarter [source: https://www.woodridgeretailgroup.com/post/unfi-deduction-pitfalls-what-to-watch-for-and-how-to-fight-back]. Scan-downs (point-of-sale-driven, no distributor markup) and bill-back MCBs (distributor pass-through, may carry markup) settle through different reconciliation flows [source: https://www.govividly.com/blog/deductions-management-tip-no-1-mcb].

## 5. Post-audit / clawback behavior

UNFI uses third-party post-audit firms that reach back **2–3 years** to surface missed deductions [source: https://tryintercept.com/blog/unfi-deductions]. UNFI's supplier terms also reserve the right to audit supplier facilities and deduct audit costs in the event of a recall or food-safety issue [source: https://www.unfi.com/supplier-terms.html].

## 6. Known quirks / gotchas

Vague chargebacks with no PO or time-period reference are routine; Woodridge cites a granola brand that lost ~1% of quarterly revenue to deductions including $7,500 of cash-discount take-downs on invoices UNFI paid five days late [source: https://www.woodridgeretailgroup.com/post/unfi-deduction-pitfalls-what-to-watch-for-and-how-to-fight-back]. The Excel-only Natural dispute form rejects screenshot/PDF submissions [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions]. Appointment scheduling differs by side: **1 day notice for Natural DCs, 3 days for Conventional** [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions]. Response times are described as "archaic" and "irregular and often quite slow" [source: https://www.spscommerce.com/community/articles/how-natural-suppliers-dispute-unfi-deductions].
