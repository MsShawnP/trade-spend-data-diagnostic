# Executive Memo: Trade Spend Diagnostic

**Cinderhaven Provisions | Trailing 12 Months**

---

## The finding

You budget 17% for trade spend. You are spending 21%. The 4-point
gap is $1 million per year in operational waste — deductions taken
by retailers beyond your negotiated rate card, largely unclassified
and uncontested.

The industry range for all-in trade spend in natural/specialty CPG
is 19-23%. Your 17.3% structural rate is competitive. Your 21.3%
all-in rate puts you at the high end — not because your rates are
wrong, but because $1M in unplanned deductions is being silently
absorbed.

## Where the money goes

| Category | Annual Cost | Addressable? |

| Vague / unclassified codes | ~$294K | Yes |

The largest single category — vague deductions with missing or
ambiguous reason codes — is $294K. You are paying $294K per year for
charges your retailers cannot specifically justify.

## What you are recovering

You filed 1,410 disputes. You recovered $98K — a 13.7% recovery
rate. Industry benchmarks for active dispute management are
25-35%. At 30% recovery on the addressable waste, you would recover
~$280K — an incremental ~$182K per year over your current rate.

## Three things to do Monday morning

1. **Dispute the vague deductions.** $294K with no clear basis.
   Request supporting documentation from each retailer. Deductions
   without a specific, documented basis are disputable — the absence
   of clear justification is itself the dispute grounds.

2. **Audit short-ship and late-delivery claims against shipping
   records.** $272K combined. Cross-reference against BOLs and
   carrier SLAs. Where the carrier missed the window, file freight
   claims. Where the warehouse shorted the order, fix the process.

3. **Fix the label compliance gap.** $197K in label fines is a
   process failure, not a retailer relationship issue. Published
   specs, pre-ship QC, updated templates.

These three actions address $763K of the $933K addressable total.

## The bottom line

Your structural trade rate is fine. Your retailers are not
overcharging you on the rate card. The problem is operational: a
million dollars per year in avoidable deductions that nobody is
classifying, nobody is contesting, and nobody is fixing the root
causes for. The diagnostic quantifies the gap. The next step is
closing it.
