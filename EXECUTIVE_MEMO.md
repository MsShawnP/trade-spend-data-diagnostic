# Executive Memo: Trade Spend Diagnostic

**Cinderhaven Provisions | Trailing 12 Months**

---

## The finding

You budget 17% for trade spend. You are spending 20%. The 4-point
gap is $1.1 million per year in operational waste — deductions taken
by retailers beyond your negotiated rate card, largely unclassified
and uncontested.

The industry range for all-in trade spend in natural/specialty CPG
is 19-23%. Your 16.7% structural rate is competitive. Your 20.4%
all-in rate is within range — but the 3.8 points of operational
waste above the rate card is money that should not be leaving the
building.

## Where the money goes

The largest single category — vague deductions with missing or
ambiguous reason codes — is $406K. You are paying over $400K per
year for charges your retailers cannot specifically justify.

## What you are recovering

You filed 3,581 disputes. You recovered $296K — an 18.6% recovery
rate. Industry benchmarks for active dispute management are
25-35%. At 30% recovery on the addressable waste, you would recover
~$327K — incremental recovery beyond the current level.

## Three things to do Monday morning

1. **Dispute the vague deductions.** $406K with no clear basis.
   Request supporting documentation from each retailer. Deductions
   without a specific, documented basis are disputable — the absence
   of clear justification is itself the dispute grounds.

2. **Audit spoilage and short-ship claims against shipping
   records.** $333K combined. Cross-reference against BOLs,
   carrier SLAs, and receiving logs. Where the carrier missed
   the window, file freight claims. Where the warehouse shorted
   the order, fix the process.

3. **Fix the label compliance gap.** $196K in label fines is a
   process failure, not a retailer relationship issue. Published
   specs, pre-ship QC, updated templates.

These three actions address the majority of the addressable total.

## The bottom line

Your structural trade rate is fine. Your retailers are not
overcharging you on the rate card. The problem is operational:
$1.1 million per year in avoidable deductions that nobody is
classifying, nobody is contesting, and nobody is fixing the root
causes for. The diagnostic quantifies the gap. The next step is
closing it.
